Enki is a marketplace where artists list encrypted prompts. Buyers pay per use to generate an image and receive the output only — never the underlying prompt, workflow, or settings.

Enki is operated as an independent project by Kevin Germin, based in Braunschweig, Germany. There is no company behind it yet; it is run by an individual. Questions: kgermin@tuta.io.

Please don’t rely on Enki for anything critical during this phase.

3. Your wallet, your funds

Enki is non-custodial. You control your own wallet and keys. Enki never takes custody of your tokens and cannot move your funds. Blockchain transactions are final and irreversible — they cannot be reversed, refunded, or charged back. You are solely responsible for securing your wallet; lost keys or access cannot be recovered by us.

4. AI-generated content

All images produced through Enki are generated by AI and are marked as such. Outputs are created by a third-party AI model, and your use of them is subject to that model provider’s terms and to applicable law. Enki makes no promise that an output is original, unique, or protectable, and claims no ownership of outputs.

We may remove any prompt, output, or account, and refuse service, at any time — especially for anything in this section. To report something, contact kgermin@tuta.io.

Prompts are the confidential property and trade secrets of the artists who list them. Buying a generation does not grant access to the prompt, and gives no exclusive rights — others may generate similar images from the same or similar prompts. Artists may remove a prompt at any time; images already generated remain usable by the buyer who made them.
